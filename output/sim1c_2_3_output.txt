  SIM 3: ENTROPY FROM ENVELOPE CONFIGURATIONS
======================================================================

  Two black holes with the same M have the same total envelope
  energy but different internal field distributions.
  Entropy = ln(number of distinct configurations).

  The configurations live on the HORIZON SHELL: the transition
  zone where phi drops from phi_max to the 1/r tail.
  Interior nodes are frozen (S4, no freedom).
  Exterior nodes are determined by Laplace (no freedom).
  Only the horizon shell has configurational freedom.

  METHOD:
  At the horizon shell (r = r_p to r_p+delta):
    - Each node has phi in range [phi_tail(r), phi_max]
    - Discretise into q = phi_max / epsilon levels (Planck quanta)
    - Total energy at shell: E_shell = sum phi_i^2
    - Number of configurations: multinomial over nodes
    - S = ln(Omega)
